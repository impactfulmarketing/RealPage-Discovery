Use Case

Impactful Apartment Marketing is a multifamily digital marketing agency. We run SEO, paid media, and reporting for a portfolio of PMC clients and their communities.

We recently built an internal dashboard to consolidate the marketing data we already work with, Google Ads, SEO, AI SEO, and Meta Ads, alongside property performance data from the PMC's PMS.

With OneSite API access, our team would see each community's current availability, occupancy, and lease-ending posture alongside its marketing performance — matching marketing spend and messaging to where each community actually is in its leasing cycle, rather than working from manual exports that are typically 7–14 days behind.

Scope of Requested Data

The integration is read-only, PMC-scoped via PmcID, and intentionally narrow — 3 methods across 2 of the 17 OneSite service categories (Units, and Pricing & Availability).

Explicitly excluded from this request

Resident PII or contact information Lease documents and signed agreements Financial transactions and payments Accounts payable and ledger data Prospect or quote pipeline data Work orders and facilities data Roommate and co-applicant data Survey responses

Requested Methods

Method Service Returns Powers in LeaseFlow
GetUnitList Pricing & Availability Available unit records with rent (BaseRent, EffectiveRent, MarketRent), availability state (Vacant, Available, MadeReady), floor plan grouping, address Community-level occupancy %, leased %, availability %; per-unit asking rent; site address
GetFloorPlans Units Floor plan metadata: name, bed/bath count, gross/rentable sq ft, market rent, occupancy-eligibility flag Floor plan card structure; per-plan occupancy calculations; sq ft breakdowns
RetrieveLeaseEndingInfo Pricing & Availability Number of leases expiring and percent allowed across configurable periods F-30 leased trend (forward-30-day occupancy outlook) — industry-standard PMS metric
Why so narrow? Minimum data needed today. Prospect Management, Quotes & Reservations, and GetRentMatrix were considered and excluded to keep surface area and confidentiality risk minimal. Expansion would be discussed with RealPage and the authorizing PMC together.

Authentication & Per-PMC Scoping

We will authenticate using the standard OneSite SOAP auth pattern with pmcid, siteid, and licensekey.

Credential scoping by design Each PMC's PmcID and LicenseKey are stored scoped to that PMC's record. Credentials are never cross-referenced between clients — enforced at the database access layer, not just at the application layer.

Logging

API integrations and authentication events are logged through our infrastructure providers, with engineering-only access to production logs.

Application logs

  • Captured via Railway's managed logging on the API server.
  • Includes operational events and errors written by our application code, such as connection refresh events, sync run outcomes, and integration call failures.
  • Logs do not include API credentials, license keys, response payloads, or other sensitive credential material.
  • Retention follows Railway's platform retention. Migration to a dedicated logging platform with extended retention is part of pre-pilot hardening.

Authentication events

  • Authentication, session, MFA, and failed login events are captured through Clerk, our identity provider (SOC 2 Type II certified).
  • MFA is enabled for Impactful staff with production access.
  • Accessible through Clerk's admin dashboard.

Database operation logs

  • Connection events, schema migrations, and operational events captured through Neon's platform.

Access controls

  • Production logs accessible only to internal Impactful engineering staff.
  • No PMC users or external parties have direct access to log data.

Pre-pilot hardening

  • Dedicated request logging middleware capturing PmcID, SiteID, authenticated user, method, endpoint, status, and duration for every OneSite integration call — separate from general application logs.
  • Extended log retention via dedicated logging platform (Better Stack, Logtail, or equivalent).
  • Configurable alerting on failed authentication thresholds and integration error patterns.

Incident Response

Scope

Applies to any security incident affecting our systems, customer data, or integration partner data. Includes unauthorized access, credential compromise, anomalous API usage, data exposure, material outages, and ransomware, malware, or denial-of-service events.

Lead

Anthony Flack currently serves as Incident Lead with responsibility for detection response, communication, and documentation. As the team scales, these responsibilities will be distributed across dedicated personnel and documented in updated versions of this plan.

Response phases

  1. Detection — via Clerk authentication alerts, Railway application logs, vendor security notifications, customer reports, or internal monitoring.
  2. Triage — severity, scope, and containment needs evaluated within 4 hours of detection.
  3. Containment — credential rotation, access revocation, traffic blocking, service isolation.
  4. Notification — affected parties notified within contractual and regulatory timeframes.
  5. Eradication — root cause identified and remediated.
  6. Recovery — affected systems restored to normal operation with verification of integrity.
  7. Post-incident review — written review within 14 days of closure, documenting what happened and what will change.

Partner and customer notification

For incidents affecting integration partner data or systems, we notify the affected partner within 24 hours of confirmation. For incidents affecting customer (PMC) data, we notify the affected customer's primary contact promptly upon confirmation. Notifications include nature of the incident, data or systems involved, known scope and impact, containment actions taken, investigation status, and expected next steps. Subsequent updates are provided as new information is confirmed.

Documentation

Every incident is logged with detection and containment times, nature of incident, systems affected, actions taken, notifications sent, and post-incident review outcomes. Logs are retained for a minimum of three years.

Data Retention & Deletion

Our retention and deletion approach for PMC operational data is outlined below. A formal written policy will be finalized as part of the API or partnership agreement with the PMC.

Active partnership retention Operational data retained on a rolling 24-month window during active partnership. Data older than 24 months is archived or deleted per the agreed schedule.
Export upon request Full data export available to the PMC at any time during the partnership, in standard formats (CSV, JSON).
Termination workflow Full data export and deletion completed per the timelines agreed upon in the API or partnership agreement with the PMC.
Backups Encrypted database backups retained per Neon platform retention policy. Backups cannot be selectively edited; PMC data in backups expires as backups age out, typically within 30 days.

Environments

LeaseFlow operates across two environments today, with a dedicated staging environment being stood up as part of pre-pilot integration hardening.

Local development Engineering laptops only. Synthetic seed data — never connected to production data. Used for feature development and unit testing.
Production Live PMC-facing environment at app.getleaseflow.ai. Hosted on Vercel (frontend) and Railway (API). Real PMC operational data stored in Neon Postgres (us-east-2). Accessible to authenticated PMC users and internal Impactful staff only.
Staging (pre-pilot) Being stood up before OneSite integration goes live. Will run on a separate Vercel project, separate Railway service, and a dedicated Neon database branch with synthetic data only. The OneSite integration sandbox credentials will be configured against staging during initial development and testing — no real PMC data will flow through staging.

Code is deployed through GitHub. Production deploys are triggered by merges to the main branch. Once staging is in place, the workflow will require every change to deploy to staging first, with manual promotion to production after validation.
